FAQ

Why are the observations reviewed?

Everyone can use Naturblick and record nature observations as he or she wishes. However, we would like to give you and other users of our platform the opportunity to assess whether the observations are plausible or not. If you wish, you can also learn how to determine your own observations more confidently and discover what exciting observations other users have made in your area.

Why are not all observations of a species on the map?

On the map you will find only observations whose species identification was at least partially confirmed. It is possible that the expert has identified a different species for some of the observations. However, the species name can be changed only by the person who has seen the animal or plant and can therefore best decide that. In the future, we will send a private note in these cases so that the creator can decide what to do with the observation. We do not change or delete these observations. We get a lot of observations every day, so it may take some time to give feedback and for the observations to appear on the map.

Is an identification wrong if it is not plausible or cannot be confirmed?

No, only the observer can really say which species he or she has seen. We can only give advice to take a closer look if necessary and to pay attention to certain characteristics. For the confirmation of an identification we need a photo or a sound recording on which the most important identification features can be seen or heard. Only then our pattern recognition or a person can give an opinion on the identification result. However, it is always difficult, even for experts, to identify species on the basis of photos or sound.

Is an identification wrong if it has not yet been confirmed?

No, we receive a lot of observations and can only gradually work on them together with volunteers. Therefore, it is possible that no one has looked at an observation yet and it still takes some time until we can give feedback.

Can the species name be changed?

Yes, the creator of an observation can change the species name if it is wrong. This actually helps us and other users of the platform. At the moment, changes can only be made in the app.

Can the details of an observation, such as location and time, be adjusted?

Yes, if the location was recorded inaccurately by the app, the user can correct it later in the app. At the moment, the time cannot be adjusted afterwards, as we assume that the Naturblick app was used directly at the time of the observation. However, the place and time of an observation can be described in more detail in the comments.

Should an observation be deleted if it is not plausible or not confirmed?

No, we are happy about every observation. Our feedback is only meant to help you and other Naturblick users if you would like to identify your observations more confidently in the future. If a user wants to delete an observation, this is of course possible. It will then no longer be visible on the map and in the personal field book. However, if it has already been used in a publication or research project, it will remain there.

What helps to be able to confirm a species identification?

Observations with a photo or an audio recording can be validated more easily. The most important identification traits should be recorded. Information for each species can be found in the species portraits. The longer the sound recording the better. Care should be taken to hold the microphone in the direction of the bird. For photos of plants, it is helpful to photograph a typical feature such as a leaf or the flower as a whole from not too far away.

How can observations be made more useful for science and conservation?

For us, it's all about the fun and fascination for nature. How and if an observation is usable always depends on the question. Therefore, we cannot give a definite answer. But there are a few general points to improve the usability:

  • Observations with photos or sound recordings allow confirmation of species identification.
  • Comments provide more details about the observations.
  • When participating in a Citizen Science project, the project's instructions on how to conduct an observation, e.g. at special times of the day, should be followed.
What species can be determined by pattern recognition?

Our algorithms are continuously improved and the number of species that can be recognized is continuously increased. Here you can find the species list for sound recognition and here for image recognition.

How does the expert confirm an identification?

The experts define traits that must be heard or seen in any case to identify a certain species. They then check the sound recordings or photographs of the observations reported as a particular species against these traits and confirm correct identifications. For observations with photos or sound recordings that do not contain these typical traits, confirmation with certainty is not possible, so the most the expert can do is give a tendency. In some cases, for example, if there is loud background noise on a sound recording or a particularly unusual specimen of a species has been photographed, even an expert cannot make an assessment based on a sound recording or a picture and will note it as such.

I would like to use these and other observations for my research. How can I use them?

All media have the license CC BY-SA 4.0, which means they may be reused. We would like to make it transparent for Naturblick users where and by whom the data was used. Therefore we ask you to contact us. Then we can give you all the information, in which context the observations were made and with which methods we validate them.
