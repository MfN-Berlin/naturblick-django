Plant recognition

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

Your photos of plants will be identified by pattern recognition. The results of the pattern recognition are generated by comparing the photo you have taken with the photos of plants that have been used to train the algorithm.

This means:

  1. only species that have been trained can be recognized,
  2. species that have not been trained can also have matches with the trained material,
  3. the recognition works best when there is a high similarity to the photo situation and quality of the training material (e.g. plants in the wild) and
  4. incorrect results may also be displayed.

Since Mai 2025, we have been using the pattern recognition from Pl@ntNet for plant recognition (available from app version 4.3.0). We always use the latest version of Pl@ntNet. More information about Pl@ntNet can be found here: www.plantnet.org/en/about/.

Until April 2025, the plant recognition was based on a pattern recognition developed and trained by us (valid for all app versions lower than 4.3.0). We used image material from naturgucker, the LIFECLEF Challenges and iNaturalist.
